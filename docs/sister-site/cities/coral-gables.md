## Common Sub-Zero Failures in Coral Gables

### What we see most on Coral Gables service calls

**1. Built-In condenser dust blockage**
Single-family Coral Gables homes get heavy yard service. Lawn equipment kicks up debris that settles into Sub-Zero condenser intakes. The compressor overworks and the unit slowly loses cooling. **Fix:** condenser cleaning, sometimes a defrost system tune-up.

**2. Pro 48 sealed-system service**
The Pro 48 is the kitchen flagship in Coral Gables luxury homes. After 15+ years, the sealed system shows its age — gradual cooling loss from microscopic refrigerant migration. We pressure-test, locate, repair, and recharge.

**3. Legacy 632 / 642 / 690 defrost board failure**
These late-90s units are still running across Coral Gables. Defrost boards fail predictably around year 20 — replacement is cheaper than the unit, restores reliable defrost cycles.

**4. Wine column humidity drift**
Coral Gables collectors with built-in wine columns face South Florida humidity pushing the units to overcompensate. RH drops below 50% and corks dry out invisibly. Recalibration restores correct humidity targeting.

**5. Ice maker line freezing in older installs**
Older Coral Gables homes have ice maker water lines run through unconditioned spaces (between cabinet and exterior wall). On cold nights — yes, even in Miami — the line can freeze. Re-routing or insulating fixes it.
