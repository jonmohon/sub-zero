## Common Sub-Zero Failures in Brickell

**1. Control board failure from voltage instability**
Brickell tower electrical systems aren't always stable — generator transitions, building maintenance cycles, and shore-power-style switching can spike or sag voltage. Sub-Zero electronic control boards die from sustained instability. We carry OEM control boards for the BI series and Designer columns.

**2. New-build condenser drywall dust accumulation**
Every Brickell tower built in the 2010-2020 wave has units with residual construction dust in Sub-Zero condenser intakes. First service call typically reveals heavy condenser blockage. Cleaning is the routine first preventive service.

**3. Built-In ice maker water inlet valve failure**
Standard failure across high-rise BI series installs — Brickell water lines are long runs through the building, often with extra inline filtering that adds restriction load on the inlet valve.

**4. Wine column compressor failures in penthouse units**
Larger Brickell penthouses often have full Sub-Zero wine rooms or multi-zone wine columns running continuous duty cycles. Compressor service at 8-10 years is the predictable next call.

**5. Door gasket degradation from constant-AC environment**
Tower units run AC at near-constant low temperature, which dries out Sub-Zero door gaskets faster than residential settings. Gasket replacement is a high-ROI repair we recommend at the 5-7 year mark.
