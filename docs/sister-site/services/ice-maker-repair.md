## Section: Common Sub-Zero Ice Maker Failures

### H2
`What's Wrong With Your Sub-Zero Ice Maker`

### Failure cards (8)

**Sub-Zero ice maker not making ice**
The most common call. Usually clogged water line, frozen fill tube, or stuck water inlet valve. Diagnostic typically under 20 minutes. Fix usually under an hour.

**Sub-Zero ice maker making small or hollow cubes**
Water flow restriction. Inlet valve partially clogged, water line kinked, or filter overdue for replacement. Easy fix.

**Sub-Zero ice maker producing cloudy or off-tasting ice**
Filter overdue (most common). Sometimes a contaminated fill tube. Replacing the filter resolves 80% of taste/appearance complaints.

**Sub-Zero ice maker leaking water**
Inlet valve stuck partially open, fill tube frozen and cracked, or drain blocked. Each has a distinctive water trail.

**Sub-Zero ice maker not dispensing ice**
Auger motor, dispenser switch, or door switch failure. Less common than production failures but easy to diagnose.

**Sub-Zero ice maker frozen up**
Ice mold over-fills and freezes solid around the harvest mechanism. Usually means the ice level sensor or the harvest motor is failing.

**Sub-Zero ice maker noise (clicking, grinding, buzzing)**
Auger motor wear, harvest motor binding, or fan obstruction. Each sound has a specific signature.

**Sub-Zero ice maker error code**
Modern units self-diagnose. We arrive with the service bulletin pulled for your error.

## Section: FAQ (8 — gets FAQPage schema)

**1. My Sub-Zero ice maker stopped making ice. What's the first thing to check?**
The water supply. Confirm the saddle valve under the sink is open and the water filter isn't past its replacement date. If both are fine, call us — most likely a stuck inlet valve or a clogged fill tube.

**2. The ice tastes weird. Is the ice maker broken?**
Usually it's the filter. Sub-Zero water filters need replacement every 6-12 months depending on water quality. South Florida water often pushes the lower end of that range.

**3. Why are my ice cubes small and hollow?**
Water flow restriction. Either the inlet valve is partially clogged, the supply line is kinked, or the filter is past replacement. Easy fix.

**4. The ice maker is making weird noises. Is that bad?**
Sometimes. A new "clicking" sound usually means the harvest motor is binding. A grinding sound usually means the auger motor is wearing out. Worth a service call before total failure.

**5. Do you fix outdoor (UC-15IO) ice makers?**
Yes. Outdoor units fail more often than indoor due to humidity exposure — we service them on the same same-day schedule.

**6. My ice maker is frozen solid inside. What now?**
Either the ice level sensor stopped reading correctly, or the harvest motor failed. We thaw, diagnose, and replace the failing component in one visit.

**7. Can I replace the water filter myself?**
Yes — Sub-Zero filters are designed for owner replacement. If the unit doesn't recognize the new filter (some require a reset), we can walk you through it on the phone.

**8. Are ice maker repairs warrantied?**
Yes. Workmanship warranty on the labor, manufacturer warranty on OEM parts. Both written on your invoice.
