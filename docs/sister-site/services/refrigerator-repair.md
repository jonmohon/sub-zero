### Failure cards (8 — keyword-rich)

**Sub-Zero refrigerator not cooling**
Compressor running, fridge warm. Most often: failed evaporator fan motor, frozen evaporator coil from defrost system failure, or sealed-system restriction. Diagnostic typically under 30 minutes.

**Sub-Zero refrigerator running constantly**
Compressor cycles never stop. Usually a failed defrost termination thermostat, condenser blocked with dust and pet hair, or door seal allowing warm-air infiltration.

**Sub-Zero ice buildup in freezer**
Frost accumulating on rear wall or floor. Defrost heater, defrost timer, or defrost thermostat failure. Common on BI-36, BI-42, and 690-series units after 8+ years of use.

**Sub-Zero refrigerator leaking water**
Pooling under or inside the unit. Usually a clogged defrost drain (most common), cracked water supply line, damaged door gasket, or — on Pro 48 — failed water inlet valve.

**Sub-Zero making noise**
Buzzing, clicking, grinding. Compressor mounts, evaporator fan blade, or condenser fan bearing. Each makes a distinctive sound a trained ear identifies in seconds.

**Sub-Zero error code (E1, E2, E3, EE, dr, etc.)**
Modern Sub-Zero units self-diagnose. We arrive with the service bulletin for your error code already pulled. Most error codes resolve in a single visit.

**Sub-Zero compressor failure**
True compressor failures are rarer than people assume. When they're real, we replace with the OEM compressor matched to your specific model — never a generic equivalent that voids the cooling architecture.

**Sub-Zero door seal / gasket replacement**
Worn gaskets force the compressor to run constantly and drive your power bill up. Gasket-only repairs are the highest-ROI service we do.

## Section: FAQ (8 entries — gets FAQPage schema)

**1. How fast can you repair my Sub-Zero refrigerator in Miami or Fort Lauderdale?**
Most Miami-Dade and Broward appointments get a same-day window if you call before noon. After-hours, weekend, and holiday appointments are scheduled at the same flat rate as weekday calls.

**2. Do you fix the older Sub-Zero models or only the new ones?**
Both. We routinely service 532, 561, 590, 632, 690, and 736 series units that are 20-30 years old. Sub-Zero designed these for repairability and we have parts.

**3. Sub-Zero says my refrigerator can't be repaired. Is that true?**
Sometimes manufacturer first-line support discontinues a part rather than replaces it. Independent specialists like us source through alternate OEM channels and rebuild components manufacturer support won't quote.

**4. What does a Sub-Zero refrigerator repair typically cost?**
Sealed-system work runs higher than fan or defrost work. We give every customer a written quote on-site before any labor begins. No work happens until you see the number and approve it.

**5. Will repair cost more than a new Sub-Zero?**
For sealed-system failures on units under 10 years old, almost always cheaper to repair. For full compressor replacements on units over 20 years old, sometimes worth considering replacement. We give honest answers either way.

**6. Do you carry Sub-Zero parts on the truck?**
Yes. The high-failure-rate components — evaporator fans, defrost boards, defrost thermostats, water inlet valves, common gaskets — ride with the technician. Special-order parts may require a follow-up visit.

**7. My Sub-Zero shows error code E1 (or E2, E3, EE, etc.). What does it mean?**
Each error code maps to a specific failure category. E1 is typically refrigerator section over-temperature. E2 is freezer section. E3 is sealed-system pressure. EE is electronic control board. We arrive with the service bulletin for your error already pulled.

**8. Are repairs warrantied?**
Yes. Workmanship warranty on the labor for every repair we perform. OEM parts carry their own manufacturer warranty on top. Both written on your invoice.
