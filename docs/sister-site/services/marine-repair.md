## Section: Why Marine Repair is Different

### H2
`Marine Refrigeration Fails Differently Than Residential`

### Lead
`A Sub-Zero in a Coral Gables kitchen and a Sub-Zero in a 65-foot yacht cabin look identical, but they fail in completely different patterns. Salt air, constant vibration, marine power systems, and tight installation envelopes all change the failure profile.`

### 4-column explanation

**Salt-air corrosion attacks contacts the manuals never mention.**
The PCB connectors inside a marine Sub-Zero corrode in 18 months instead of 15 years. Compressor terminals, sensor leads, and door sensors all get the salt treatment. We carry corrosion-replacement contact sets standard.

**Vibration loosens what gravity holds.**
Refrigerant lines that would never shift in a kitchen install slowly walk loose under constant marine vibration. Refrigerant slow-leaks present as gradual cooling loss over months — easy to misdiagnose as compressor weakness.

**Marine power isn't grid power.**
Yacht inverters and shore-power converters introduce voltage instability that residential units never see. Sub-Zero electronic control boards aren't always tolerant. We diagnose with marine-grade meters, not residential ones.

**Installation envelopes are tight.**
Service access in a yacht galley is not service access in a kitchen. We bring the tools — short-handle wrenches, magnetic retrievers, articulating mirrors — that the residential techs don't carry.

## Section: Common Marine Sub-Zero Failures

### H2
`What We See on Marine Sub-Zero Calls`

### Failure cards (8)

**Marine refrigerator gradual cooling loss**
Slow refrigerant leak from vibration-loosened service ports or connections. Most common marine failure. We pressure-test, locate, repair, and recharge in one visit.

**Marine ice maker stops producing**
Salt-air corrosion on water inlet valve contacts, or fill tube freeze due to inverter voltage drop. Distinctive marine signature.

**Marine refrigerator electronic control board failure**
Voltage spikes from inverter switching or shore-power transitions kill control boards faster than residential equivalents. We carry OEM replacements and surge-protect at install.

**Marine refrigerator door seal failure**
Vibration + flexing + salt air = door seal degradation 3x faster than residential. Easy fix, high ROI.

**Marine compressor terminal corrosion**
Salt creep into compressor electrical connections. Looks like compressor failure, is actually contact corrosion. Quick fix if caught early; expensive if missed.

**Marine condenser fan failure**
Salt-air bearing degradation. Squealing or seized fan. We carry marine-grade replacements.

**Marine refrigerator freezing food in the fridge section**
Sensor drift due to corroded leads. Replacing the sensor is a 30-minute fix.

**Marine wine cooler temperature instability**
Dual issue: vibration-induced refrigerant migration + voltage instability. We address both at once.

## Section: Marine FAQ (10 — gets FAQPage schema)

**1. Do you service Sub-Zero marine units at every South Florida marina?**
Most. Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach marinas — same-day. Naples, Marco Island, Stuart — typically next-day. Florida Keys marinas — extended scheduling, but yes.

**2. What's different about marine Sub-Zero repair vs. residential?**
Failure patterns, parts inventory, tools, voltage diagnostics. A residential technician can't service a marine unit reliably without marine-specific parts and equipment. Worth verifying any contractor you hire actually does marine work.

**3. My yacht is in dry storage. Can you still service it?**
Yes. Dry storage actually makes service easier — no slip access coordination, no power-management considerations.

**4. Do you charge extra for marina service vs. residential?**
No. Same flat rate. Marine slip-access requirements occasionally add a coordination buffer to scheduling, but the labor rate is identical.

**5. Can you handle the Florida Keys marinas?**
Yes, but with longer scheduling windows. Travel time to Marathon or Key West means we typically schedule a half-day. Worth it for full-service marine work.

**6. What happens when shore power switches to inverter? Will my Sub-Zero be okay?**
Sub-Zero units are designed to handle most power transitions, but voltage instability over time degrades electronic control boards. We can install surge protection during a routine service.

**7. My boat's Sub-Zero ice maker stopped — is the corrosion always behind it?**
Often. Salt-air ingress on the water inlet valve contacts is the #1 marine ice maker failure. We replace the contact set as preventive maintenance during ice maker service.

**8. Do you service Sub-Zero units on commercial vessels (charter boats, sportfishing)?**
Yes. Same dispatch, same flat rate. Commercial customers often book preventive maintenance schedules — worth a conversation if you operate a vessel commercially.

**9. Can you provide a service report for insurance purposes?**
Yes. Detailed written service report on request — many marine insurance carriers ask for documentation after refrigeration repair claims.

**10. What's the longest you've kept a marine Sub-Zero running?**
We routinely service marine Sub-Zero units 15-20 years old that are still on their original compressor. Marine units treated well outlast their residential cousins because owners notice problems faster.
