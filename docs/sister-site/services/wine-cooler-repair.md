## Section: Common Wine Cooler Failures

### H2
`What Goes Wrong With Sub-Zero Wine Coolers`

### Lead
`Wine columns fail differently than refrigerators because the temperature window is tighter and the humidity tolerance is narrower. The same diagnostic shortcuts work across the active product lines.`

### Failure cards (8)

**Sub-Zero wine cooler not cold enough**
Temperature drifting up. Most likely failed evaporator fan, compressor cycling issue, or door seal failure. Diagnostic typically under 30 minutes.

**Sub-Zero wine column temperature fluctuating between zones**
Dual-zone column with one zone drifting. Usually the zone-specific evaporator fan or a damper between zones. Easy to isolate.

**Sub-Zero wine cooler humidity too low (corks drying out)**
Door seal compromised or evaporator running too cold. Cork desiccation is a real loss event — service immediately.

**Sub-Zero wine cooler vibrating**
Compressor mounts worn, condenser fan loose, or the unit is on uneven flooring. Vibration agitates wine and accelerates aging.

**Sub-Zero wine cooler clicking or buzzing**
Compressor relay, condenser fan bearing, or evaporator fan blade hitting ice buildup. Distinctive sounds, fast diagnostic.

**Sub-Zero wine cooler door seal failure**
Worn gasket lets warm air in. Forces the compressor to overwork and pushes humidity out. High-ROI repair.

**Sub-Zero wine cooler interior light not working**
LED driver failure on Designer wine columns, bulb on legacy units. Quick fix.

**Sub-Zero wine cooler error code**
Modern units self-diagnose. We arrive with the service bulletin pulled.

## Section: Why Recalibration Matters

### H2
`Why We Recalibrate After Every Service — Not Just Compressor Work`

### Lead
`Wine columns can drift their target temperature by 2-3°F after any electrical or sealed-system service. Most repair shops don't recalibrate unless the customer complains. We recalibrate every time, automatically. Here's why.`

### 3-column explanation

**The 2-degree problem.**
A wine column set to 55°F running at 57°F doesn't trigger an alarm. The owner won't notice. But a vintage Bordeaux at 57°F vs 55°F ages roughly 30% faster. Over months, that's a meaningful loss.

**The humidity problem.**
Sub-Zero wine columns target 50-70% relative humidity. After service, RH often drifts to 35-40% — invisible to the owner, slow death for corks. We measure and adjust before we leave.

**The recalibration process.**
Bring the unit to set-point. Wait 30 minutes. Verify with a calibrated thermocouple at three shelf positions. Verify RH with a calibrated hygrometer. Adjust the control board to compensate for drift. Document on the invoice.

**2. My wine column shows the right temperature on the display but the wine feels warm. What's wrong?**
The display reads air temperature near the sensor. If the bottle temp is different, the sensor itself is drifting — needs recalibration or replacement.

Yes. Indicates humidity has dropped below 50%. Usually a door seal failure or evaporator running too cold. Service quickly to limit cork damage.
